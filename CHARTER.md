# Mission and Principles

## Mission

Public Knowledge Foundation works to expand access to reliable, understandable, reusable knowledge and to strengthen people's capacity to participate in public life.

## Principles

1. Knowledge is a public capability, not merely a commodity.
2. Openness must be matched by care for privacy, safety, accessibility, and attribution.
3. Accuracy includes the ability to show sources, limits, and correction history.
4. Civic learning should increase agency without prescribing partisan allegiance.
5. Governance must be transparent enough to earn trust and humble enough to change.

## Scope

The Foundation may support educational materials, research, software, community practice, translation, and public-interest infrastructure consistent with this mission.
