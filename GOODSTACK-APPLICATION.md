# Goodstack Application Draft

## Application status

**Preparation only.** Public Knowledge Foundation must first be legally registered in an eligible jurisdiction. KnowledgeCivic is the open-source civic-education project operated or proposed by the organization; it is not, by itself, proof of nonprofit status.

## Organization profile

- Legal organization name: `Public Knowledge Foundation` (use the exact registered name)
- Public project: `KnowledgeCivic`
- Website / public record: `https://github.com/KnowledgeCivic`
- Mission: expand access to reliable, understandable, reusable knowledge and strengthen people's capacity to participate in public life.
- Program: provide open civic-learning materials, source-based public knowledge packs, media and information literacy lessons, and community research methods.
- Beneficiaries: learners, educators, community groups, and the wider public, with emphasis on people who face barriers to formal education or reliable information.
- Political status: non-partisan and not operated for private benefit.

## Documents to prepare

- Government-issued nonprofit registration certificate.
- Tax-exemption or charitable-status confirmation, if applicable.
- Official nonprofit-register extract showing the exact legal name and registration number.
- Governing document or charter showing the public-benefit purpose.
- Official organization email address and proof of affiliation for the applicant.
- Bank-account ownership documents only when applying to receive donations.

## Submission checklist

1. Confirm the legal entity is registered and active.
2. Visit [Goodstack sign-up](https://causes.goodstack.org/).
3. Select the country where the nonprofit is registered.
4. Search the exact legal name or registry ID.
5. Claim the matching organization, then use a work email address.
6. Upload only relevant official documents with personal data redacted where permitted.
7. Keep copies of the submitted materials and verification correspondence.

## Accuracy rule

Do not submit the GitHub project name as the legal nonprofit name unless it is also the registered legal name. Do not describe the organization as registered, tax-exempt, or operationally active until the relevant authority has confirmed that status.

## Draft public description

Public Knowledge Foundation is a proposed non-profit public-interest organization developing KnowledgeCivic, an open civic-learning commons. The project helps people understand public questions, evaluate evidence, learn about rights and institutions, and contribute to knowledge that others can verify and reuse. Materials are published openly, with sources, uncertainty, correction history, accessibility considerations, and clear licensing.
